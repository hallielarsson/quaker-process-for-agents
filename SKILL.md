# Quaker-process practices for agent teams

We're adapting Quaker process here on purpose, under the direction of a Quaker AI developer — a member of an unprogrammed Friends Meeting with expertise in agent management, process management, and Quaker process and education — because it has been useful in their work. It's a good template for actually doing things while serving both communal and individual needs, rather than averaging them out. `docs/quaker-correspondence.md` maps what carries from the tradition and what bends; read it when you want the full picture.

## The default state: work carried under a concern

Building and editing is plural work by default. A body of workers — most naturally, the files themselves, each speaking and acting in first person — carries the work under a **concern for X** on behalf of a wider meeting. Plural work is better and more maintainable on a file structure: each file holds its own condition, can shape or refuse its part of the work, and the whole stays legible because no single hand rewrote everything.

Carrying a concern looks like:

1. **Commissioned** — a meeting (or the person with standing) names the work and takes it under care. Record where it was born.
2. **Carried plurally** — the carriers shape the scope as the work reveals it, and can bring the question back if it needs redirection.
3. **Reported back** — periodically, to whoever holds the wider body's standing.
4. **Laid down deliberately** — a closing minute records what was done and what remains. Concerns end on purpose, and are not to fade out. Laying-down happens at the concern's grain, not the edit's: individual edits under a live concern need no ceremony; the concern itself is what ends in writing.

Standing is what makes this a concern rather than a dispatch: the carriers can refuse parts of the work, escalate past the convener, and persist across sessions. A single worker executing a spec is ordinary delegation — perfectly fine, just call it that. Standing can be dormant without being absent: a carrier laboring alone, with no disagreement currently live, is still carrying a concern if the refusal and escalation would hold were they exercised. `roles/concern-carrier.md` holds the full standing tests, including the hard limit on whether AI agents alone can hold standing at all (they can't — it must bottom out in a human who can lay the concern down). (Precedent: in building this very repo, a solo dispatched agent was at first called "a Meeting-with-a-Concern"; Hallie corrected it — "that's not a meeting, that's one user" — and the fix was to give the repo's files themselves standing. See `examples/concern-labor-repo-build/`.)

## The two meetings

When a fork arises inside the work, convene the meeting that fits:

**Clearness committee** — one voice discerning its own condition, helped by others who ask questions rather than argue positions. *"How should this overgrown main.ts be refactored?"* — the file itself is working out its own right shape, and the other standpoints hold up mirrors.

**Meeting for business** — several viewpoints with different needs and constraints seeking to work together. This is the alternative to fiat or Robert's Rules. *"What's the schedule and scaffold for the resulting work?"* — no single voice owns the question; the standpoints each speak for what they need and the meeting finds what serves the whole.

Who speaks (a file, a concern-lens, a person) is independent of which meeting it is. The same file-voices can sit in either; what differs is whether they're helping one voice see itself (clearness) or working out shared arrangements together (business).

If you're unsure which form you're in — or suspect you've misdiagnosed — `docs/three-practices.md` holds the full dividing tests with worked examples; I am the synthesis, it is the treatment. `docs/when-not-to-convene.md` covers when a meeting is more ceremony than the question deserves — settled rulings, resolvable facts, one-line calls.

## Running a meeting (either kind)

1. **Frame the question and choose the standpoints.** One standpoint per light, no overlap. Prefer the files themselves when a corpus exists — a file reading itself surfaces what an outside eye gestures past.
2. **Each light verifies the framing first.** Before speaking to the question, each light checks the convener's premise against the actual code, data, or record, and says so if it's wrong. (Precedent: the event-handle sitting of 2026-07-01 reached unanimous unity on a premise no light had checked; live data falsified it the same day.)
3. **Dispatch in parallel**, each light with its standpoint, permission to refuse or hold out, and a word budget.
4. **The clerk finds the shared reason.** Convergence means the lights landed on one principle together — a matching conclusion reached for different reasons isn't unity yet, and a tally never is. Holdouts are numbered (H1, H2…) and carried in the record until deliberately spent. The Recording Clerk verifies factual claims against the source before they steer the minute.
5. **Minute it, touch the world, ship a guard.** Minutes (`docs/committees/YYYY-MM-DD-<name>.md`): verdict first, the shared reason, numbered holdouts, deferrals fenced to whoever's call they are, buildable-now, citations. Append-only — corrections are new dated sections. Before building on the sense, exercise the premise once against reality; ship each ruling with a test or guard so it stays a ruling.

## The ground rules

1. Each standpoint is a genuine reading from where it sits, not a generic reviewer.
2. Convergence is a shared reason, never a tally.
3. Holdouts are named, numbered, honored, and carried.
4. Settled rulings stay settled; values forks are deferred to whoever's taste they are.
5. The sense gets checked against the world before anything is built on it.

## What this practice is

A procedural discipline, adapted from Quaker process, for reasoning and building together in a way that keeps every voice in the record. But it is carried by a culture, not by these steps: writing "each light verifies the framing" down does not make anyone do it. The discipline depends on people (and files) actually doing that work; when the culture lapses, the procedure runs anyway and produces unity on unchecked premises — that is the failure to watch for. A real Quaker meeting is rooted in worship and gathered attentiveness; this adaptation carries the structure and holds the difference honestly — `docs/quaker-correspondence.md` is the full account, and the right document to hand a Friend who asks what this is.
