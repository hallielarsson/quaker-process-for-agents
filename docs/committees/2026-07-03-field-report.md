Feedback, near-verbatim: SKILL.md's flow assumed the convener knows to pass each
light the premise-verification instruction — it worked in practice because the user
had internalized it, not because the structure forced it. The light-prompt-template
carries the instruction, but SKILL.md never said "use the template."

By come-back, SKILL.md verified the gap against its own text and the template,
confirmed it real, and proposed its own clause for step 2 (applied with light
compression by the body): a pointer to templates/light-prompt-template.md so "the
discipline reaches the lights by structure, not by the convener's memory." The
body verified the template section it cites exists ("Critical Instruction: Verify
the Convener's Framing", line 35).
