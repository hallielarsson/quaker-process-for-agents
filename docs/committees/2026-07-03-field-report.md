# First field report: the practice in live use — 2026-07-03 (evening)

**Sense:** The practice ran across three repos in one afternoon without bending, its
most valuable moment was telling its user NOT to use it, and one real gap was found
and closed by come-back. Feedback came from the convener using the practice on live
work (scher, lingit prototype, a consumer repo); recorded here as field evidence.

## What worked

- **when-not-to-convene.md as a stopping-gate.** A holdout (H1 in scher's
  quality-extensibility minutes) looked like committee material; the resolvable-fact
  rule sent the user to check three databases first, which settled it without a
  sitting. The doc did its highest work: preventing ceremony.
- **A merged business sitting caught what a solo build would have missed.** Five
  lights across two repos: one sketched auto-applying contractions; another (a
  governance doc) held that auto-apply makes a choice for the human. The
  reconciliation — rules propose, human confirms — became the shipped design's
  center, and the tool's owner then independently asked for exactly that proposer
  UI. Unity as shared reason, confirmed by the world.

## The gap, and its closing

Feedback, near-verbatim: SKILL.md's flow assumed the convener knows to pass each
light the premise-verification instruction — it worked in practice because the user
had internalized it, not because the structure forced it. The light-prompt-template
carries the instruction, but SKILL.md never said "use the template."

By come-back, SKILL.md verified the gap against its own text and the template,
confirmed it real, and proposed its own clause for step 2 (applied with light
compression by the body): a pointer to templates/light-prompt-template.md so "the
discipline reaches the lights by structure, not by the convener's memory." The
body verified the template section it cites exists ("Critical Instruction: Verify
the Convener's Framing", line 35).
