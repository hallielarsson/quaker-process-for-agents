# Meeting for business: consensus on the Ponytail-style repo — 2026-07-03 (evening)

**Sense of the meeting:** One hard rule, a few checkable conventions beneath it, fenced
disclaimers around it, in a repo small enough to obey itself. Reached by three lights
(the seconded testimony voice, the stewardship engineer's eye, the anti-theater gate),
each writing IN the register it proposed.

**The shared reason:** plainness is measured by HONESTY PER TOKEN, not simplicity of
grammar. All three lights independently held: precision is usually the cheaper honest
form; the integrity boundary is a rule, not an exception; and the ruleset must obey
itself — a rule that cannot be written plainly is evidence against the rule.

**Working assumption, named and correctable by Hallie:** "Ponytail" read as the xkcd
character — terse, blunt, technically precise; plainness WITH precision, where
CAVEMAN/grug is plainness with affect (which can spend tokens: "grug think complexity
bad" carries less per token than "avoid complexity").

## The consensus shape

**Ground floor — one hard rule (the gate's, adopted whole):**
> Say what you mean. No honorifics, status-language, or jargon masks. Call things by
> their names. Explain acronyms once. If you do not know, say so. If the honest
> account is complex, be complex — do not simplify it away.

**Conventions beneath it (the engineer's, adopted as checkable practices, labeled
conventions — never testimony):**
1. Verdict-first: the first sentence answers "what should I do?"
2. No hedge-stacking: one uncertainty marker per claim; a hedge must mark real doubt.
3. No context-restating: write for the reader who holds the context; strike what a
   linked file already says.
4. Measure, don't police: token audits on substantive rewrites (target ~15% reduction
   at equal information); exceptions documented inline, never punished.

**Fences (the testimony light's and the gate's, adopted whole):**
- The integrity boundary as a stated rule: never buy simplicity with precision;
  break register whenever precision or honest uncertainty requires, without apology.
- The non-claim, in the README: these rules are a speech practice, not the Quaker
  testimony; plainness in the tradition grows from a ground these rules do not
  embody and must not claim.
- Where the register does NOT apply: specifications and contract language; apologies
  and amends; minutes of wrongs; quoted testimony in another's voice.
- Anti-metastasis: keep the rule-set smaller than seems sufficient; add a rule only
  for a demonstrated failure, and let the ruleset stay subject to its own rules.

**Shape:** small repo — README (<=200 words), RULES.md, one before/after example pair
with token counts, an audit checklist of three questions. No linter.

**Name:** `ponytail-protocol` (two lights). H1 below carries the third's dissent.
