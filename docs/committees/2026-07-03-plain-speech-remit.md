# Meeting for business: plain-speech repo rules — remit, participation, merit — 2026-07-03

**Sense of the meeting:** (1) NOT our remit — this repo teaches process forms, not voice
or register. (2) YES, we would gladly participate in authoring it elsewhere, as a
seconded light, with one boundary. (3) A qualified good idea: good insofar as it means
the testimony (refusing jargon-masks, hedges, and status-language), risky insofar as it
mandates a simple register.

**The shared reason, reached independently by all three lights:** plain speech as a
testimony is about HONESTY — removing fake layers (honorifics, flattery, hedging, jargon
masking no-answer) — not about simplified grammar. A mandated simple register risks
being the costume of plainness rather than the practice: it can hide precision, perform
confidence, and amputate nuance, which is against the truth-telling the discernment
practice depends on. The testimony removes false complexity; it does not remove real
complexity.

Convened on Hallie's question, verbatim: "Can you ask that repository to consider
whether plain-speech as a CAVEMAN or Ponytail style repo rules is within their remit,
whether they would like to participate in the authoring if not and whether they thing
it is a good idea?" ("CAVEMAN style" read as the grug-brained-developer genre;
"Ponytail style" carried as honest ignorance — not guessed; see the question back.)

## The lights

- **quaker-correspondence.md** (the testimony): plain speech meant refusing honorifics
  and status-language — equality and transparency, not simple vocabulary. Its crispest
  evidence: grug's "complexity very bad" versus "this design incurs a maintenance cost
  that compounds faster than the problem it solves" — the second is grammatically more
  complex and MORE honest; the first performs wisdom it does not hold. A guide may
  contain both a real testimony-clause (no jargon-masks, explain acronyms, call things
  by their names) and a style convention (short words, simple grammar) — but must name
  them differently and never claim the style convention IS the Quaker testimony.
- **SKILL.md** (remit and register): adjacent, not remit — "I teach how to hold a
  question together, not how to speak it." Stewarding a register standard from inside
  would blur what the repo is: "better a seconded light than a confused clerk." Noted
  a real register problem the ecosystem has (abstract language that reads settled when
  unverified — the event-handle failure had a register dimension), so the work is good
  work; it is just not ours to hold.
- **when-not-to-convene.md** (the performance risk): a mandated register agents did not
  choose can hide precision, perform confidence, and misrepresent process ("thinking is
  not simple; pretending it is is dishonest"). If the true goal is accessibility or
  parseability, cheaper fixes exist (templates, role-naming, bracketed asides). If the
  goal is sounding folksy or trust-building, it is theater. Whatever is authored, agents
  must be free to break the register when precision or honest uncertainty requires it.

## The three answers, plainly

1. **Remit:** No. This repo stays a process repo.
2. **Participation:** Yes, gladly — a light seconded to the authoring body's sittings
   (the way edge_word.rs sat in the merged meeting), carrying the testimony/style
   distinction above. Boundary: we will not endorse a document that claims mandated
   simple register IS plain-speech testimony, or that forbids breaking register for
   precision.
3. **Good idea:** Yes, if it is a clarity-and-honesty standard with the register as an
   optional convention. No, if it is a register mandate wearing the testimony's name.

## HALLIE'S GOALS, RECEIVED — 2026-07-03 (minutes after the sitting)

Hallie, verbatim: "My GOALS with it are specifically that it 1) reduces tokens so is in
line with stewardship 2) plain speech, for me, is an expression of the intersection of
simplicity and integrity and in the service of equality and peace."

The meeting's reception: this strengthens the sense rather than overturning it. Goal (1)
joins the stewardship testimony SKILL.md named in itself this same day ("parsimonious
with resources") — token-parsimony is already our practice, and a register in its
service is coherent, not costume. Caution carried from the lights: caveman AFFECT does
not always minimize tokens ("grug think complexity bad" spends more than "avoid
complexity"); if stewardship is the goal, the target is terse precision, measured
against actual token counts. Goal (2) is the testimony-reading the correspondence light
gave, in Hallie's own key: under it, the standard's hard rule writes itself —
simplicity may never be bought with integrity; agents stay free to break register for
precision or honest uncertainty. The remit and participation answers stand unchanged.

Hallie, further, verbatim: "Also 'fewer moving parts, the thing says what it does' is
genuinely good code and system design practice." Received: this extends the testimony
from prose into structure — a self-describing system is plain speech practiced in
design, and it is where the stewardship argument compounds (fewer tokens in every
future reading, not just in the rules file).

Hallie, further, verbatim: "also self documenting code == let your life speak --
integrity of the code more than brittle comments and stale documentation -- and also
serves conways law / beer's 2nd order cybernetics". Received: the testimony was never
"attach an accurate description to your life" — the living is the witness, so code whose
behavior is its own testimony cannot drift from its account the way stale docs do
(integrity as being one thing, not a thing plus a claim about it). Conway: structure
speaks regardless; the choice is whether it speaks honestly. Beer: a system that can
read itself can regulate itself — which is what files-speaking-in-first-person already
practices here. A file that can say what it is, is a file that can sit in a meeting.

Hallie, completing the set, verbatim: "the third point there is that -- complex,
self-modifying systems tend to exhibit self similarity as they are both a society in
whitehead terms and also have nonlinear dynamics". Received: a persisting self-modifying
system is a Whiteheadian society (a pattern inheriting itself through its own occasions),
and nonlinearity under iteration is the regime that yields self-similarity — the same
form recurring at every scale. This day is the evidence: one practice ran unchanged at
the sentence scale (a two-word title come-back), the file scale (a clearness sitting),
the repo scale (a business meeting), and across three repos (merged sittings). The
consequence for register: in a self-similar system, whatever is practiced at one scale
propagates — honest structure begets honest structure, and masks beget masks, fractally.
That is why plain speech here is load-bearing rather than stylistic.
