## Practice-specific notes

### Clearness committee minutes

- The verdict describes what the light (the one participant holding the leading) came to see about their own condition, not what the querying body thinks they should do.
- Holdouts are concerns raised by the light about their own discernment, or points where the querying body's questions surfaced conflicting leadings within the light. They are not the querying body taking a contrary position.
- Deferrals are taste calls the light cannot resolve alone and defers to another person or role.
- The shared reason is what helped the light see more clearly — a principle or question that opened something, or a premise the light was standing on that the queries helped them examine.

### Business meeting minutes

- The verdict is the body's reading of its own shared matter — what the process has discerned about the decision at hand.
- Holdouts are standpoints whose reading remains unresolved or partly unresolved. They are carried in the record until they are explicitly spent (a later decision, a fact that settled the question, or an agreed-upon way of holding the tension).
- Deferrals are values forks the body as a whole cannot resolve on facts — places where the right answer depends on whose taste is being served, or what the thing *is* as opposed to what it does.
- The shared reason is the principle both/all branches of the process landed on, read through their different lenses.

### Concern-labor minutes

- The verdict describes what was completed, what remains open, and (critically) who besides the laborer is holding or reviewing the concern.
- A blank or "only the laborer" answer to "who is holding the concern" is itself diagnostic information: it names the gap in plurality, not something to smooth over.
- Holdouts are obstacles the labor team encountered that they cannot resolve alone — dependencies, scope creep, needed decisions from the wider body.
- Deferrals are decisions the wider Meeting must make before the labor can proceed.
- The shared reason is the principle the labor team discerned about the work they are holding.
